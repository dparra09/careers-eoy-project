What Goes Where
Board Panel Guide

Each panel below maps to a section of your tri-fold board. Click through for requirements.

🏥
Title Panel
Top Center · Front
10 pts

The first thing visitors see. Make it clear and professional.

  • Department name at 1–2" height
  • Facility location at ¾–1" height
  • Text centered on the panel
🪪
Student Information
Bottom Front Center + Top Back Center
5 pts

Two separate placements — front and back of the center panel.

  • Front bottom: Name, Teacher, Period
  • Back top: Teacher, Period, Facility, Department
📋
Mission & Vision
Center Panel · Below Title
10 pts

Use your site's official statements — then rewrite them in your own words.

  • Official Mission Statement from your site
  • Official Vision Statement from your site
  • Summarized in your own words
  • No personal opinions or explanations
Find these on your site's website or ask your supervisor.
📸
Visuals
Bottom Center Panel
15 pts

Show what your site looks like and what it does through images.

  • At least 3 color photos of the site in action
  • 1 photo of YOU in professional attire at the site
  • No clip art — real photos only
  • Get supervisor approval for all photos
  • No photos of children or patients without written consent
💼
Site Careers
Top Left Panel
15 pts

Research the careers found at your site and describe each one.

  • One full typed page of careers
  • Use career titles only (e.g., "Nurse Practitioner")
  • Researched description for each role
  • No names or pseudonyms of real people
Use the BLS Occupational Outlook Handbook for research: bls.gov/ooh
🗺️
Physical Space
Bottom Left Panel
15 pts

Create a color floor plan and describe the layout of your site.

  • Labeled, color schematic (try Floorplanner.com)
  • 1–2 typed paragraphs describing the layout
Label key rooms, stations, or areas your schematic includes.
📌
Artifact
Top Right Panel
15 pts

One real item from your site that represents what the site does.

  • Examples: brochure, glove, form, badge lanyard, etc.
  • Must be clean, unused, and non-valuable
  • Get supervisor approval before removing anything
  • Include a brief written explanation of the item
When in doubt, ask your supervisor first.
✍️
Personal Acknowledgment
Bottom Right Panel
15 pts

Reflect on your fieldwork experience in a full typed page.

  • One typed page — be genuine and professional
  • You may thank individuals by name and title (optional)
  • Keep tone positive and professional
🖥️
Google Slide
Submitted Separately
15 pts

A single Google Slide that visually summarizes your entire board.

  • One slide only — submitted separately from the board
  • Used for your in-class presentation
  • Must match the quality and content of your board
Think of it as the "cover" of your project — make it count.

Before You Print
Key Reminders

Commonly missed requirements that affect your grade.

Plan before you glue

Lay everything out dry on the board before attaching anything. No visible tape, staples, or glue sticks — use a glue runner or spray adhesive for clean edges.

Border every component

Each section should have a printed or cut border using 1–3 contrasting colors. This counts under Organization, Neatness, and Creativity (15 pts).

All text must be typed

Handwriting is not accepted anywhere on the board. Use clear section titles (e.g., "Site Goals") so reviewers can navigate your board easily.

Supervisor approval is mandatory

Photos and artifacts both require supervisor sign-off. Do this before your last site visit — do not wait until the night before it's due.

Your photo must be from the intro assignment

The "Picture of You" must be the same image submitted in your personal introduction assignment — not a selfie taken later.

Google Slide ≠ a screenshot of your board

Design it to be readable on a screen. Use readable font sizes and arrange content so it looks intentional — not just a shrunken version of your board.
